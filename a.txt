Project: Over-The-Air (OTA) Update Success Prediction and Optimization
Problem Statement
In modern connected vehicles, software updates are delivered Over-The-Air (OTA). The process is complex and often fails due to external factors (e.g., weak signal, low battery, user interruption) or internal software dependencies. Failed OTA updates lead to increased warranty costs, user frustration, and potential safety/security vulnerabilities.

The challenge is to predict the probability of an OTA update failure for a specific vehicle before the update is initiated, and to determine the optimal conditions (e.g., time, location) to maximize the success rate.

Technical Focus
This project leverages Classification Modeling, Contextual Data Analysis, and Optimization to address a critical operational challenge for Volvo.

Data Needed (Simulated or Public)
Historical OTA Log Data:

Outcome: Success (1) or Failure (0) of the update.

Contextual Variables: Update size, firmware version being installed, and vehicle model.

Telemetry Data at Time of Update (T-0):

Vehicle State: Battery State of Charge (SoC), Ignition Status, Engine/Motor Temperature.

Connectivity: Signal Strength (RSSI) of the cellular modem, Network Type (4G/5G).

Usage: Distance driven since last maintenance, current time of day, day of the week.

Environmental Data (Geospatial lookup):

Location: City/Highway, Altitude, Proximity to known cell towers (simulated).

Key Steps & Deliverables
Feature Engineering & Data Prep:

Clean and merge the historical logs with the telemetry data based on the update timestamp.

Discretize continuous variables (e.g., group SoC into low/medium/high).

Predictive Model Training:

Use a Classification Algorithm (e.g., Gradient Boosting, XGBoost, or Logistic Regression) to predict the Outcome (Success/Failure).

Identify the Feature Importances to show which factors (e.g., Battery SoC, Signal Strength) are the strongest predictors of failure.

Optimization Recommendation:

Based on your model, define a set of rules that maximize the success probability (e.g., "Only initiate updates when SoC is > 70% AND Signal Strength is > -90 dBm").

Deliverable: Create a simple Decision Table for an OTA scheduling system.

Presentation/Report:

Quantify the potential reduction in failed updates and the resulting cost savings (e.g., reduction in dealership service visits for manual flashing).

Emphasize how this project enhances the Customer Experience and Vehicle Quality.
